Description

The Doctor of Strategic Christian Leadership is a 51-semester-hour program designed to help students strengthen and refine leadership abilities grounded in Biblical servant leadership principles. Learners apply these principles to ministry, business, and nonprofit contexts to lead with vision, integrity, and strategic wisdom.

This program combines biblical insight, leadership theory, and organizational management, equipping students to assess, plan, implement, and evaluate leadership strategies that transform organizations and communities for the glory of God.

Program Learning Outcomes

  • Evaluate key skills, resources, and content required for successful leadership.
  • Utilize leadership principles to develop strategic plans for organizations.
  • Integrate biblical and ethical principles within one’s leadership practice.
  • Evaluate and define personal leadership style rooted in theological and moral principles.
  • Acquire competencies to become an effective, Christ-centered strategic leader.

  • Research Conceptualization and Design

  • Leadership in Contemporary Organizations
  • Change Management
  • Entrepreneurial Leadership
  • Data-Informed Decision Making
  • Transformation and Leading Change
  • Social Justice and Faith: Forming the Beloved Community
  • Elements of Marketing & Branding
  • Finance and Resource Management
  • The Principles of Christocracy (2 Semester Hours)
